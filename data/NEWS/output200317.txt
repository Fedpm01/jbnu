NU is expanding its ‘CLS (Critical Language Scholarship) program’. CBNU was selected to conduct this program by the U.S. State Department of Educational and Cultural Affairs (DECA) starting last year. After DECA chose Korean as one of the 13 remarkable world languages, the CBNU Language Education Center was selected as the only official institution in Korea last year. aTo discuss about expanding this CLS program, American Council Vice-chairman David Patton, who in charge of this business, visited CBNU. According to the discussion regarding language education, CBNU decided to conduct home-stay and real-life experiences of Korean culture. aAlso, working groups in the Language Education Center are going to visit America.
